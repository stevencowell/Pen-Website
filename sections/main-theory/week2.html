Begin by selecting straightâ€‘grained timber and cutting it to length. Mark a pencil line across the blank before sawing it into two pieces so you can realign the grain later. Drill a hole through the centre of each piece using the size specified in your pen kit, retracting the drill frequently to clear swarf. Scuff the brass tubes with sandpaper, apply glue and insert them into the blanks. Once the glue dries, square the ends so the wood is flush with the tubes.

Mounting on the lathe

Slide a bushing onto the mandrel, add one pen blank, a centre bushing, the second blank and a final bushing. Tighten the thumb nut gently so everything is snug but not overtightened. Mount the mandrel in the lathe and support the end with the tailstock. Align the grain marks so that the pattern will match when the pen is assembled.

Turning â€“ Shaping the pen bodies

Turn on the lathe and use a sharp spindle gouge or roughing gouge to round the blanks. Work slowly, holding the tool firmly on the tool rest and taking light cuts. Once round, shape the profile of each blank. A common design is slightly thicker in the middle and flush with the bushings at the ends. Take care not to cut too deeply near the bushings.

Sand the pen blanks on the lathe, moving through progressively finer grits (e.g.Â 120, 180, 240, 320, 400, 600) until the surface is smooth. Wipe off dust between grits. Apply a finish such as friction polish or wax while the lathe spins slowly, taking care to keep the cloth flat and away from moving parts.

Assembly â€“ Putting the pen together

Lay out the pen components: the nib (tip), the clip and end cap, the internal mechanism, the ink refill and any decorative rings. Press the nib into one blank, insert the mechanism to the correct depth and test that it actuates the refill. Press the clip onto the other blank. Align the grain and press the two halves together. Finally, test the pen to ensure it operates smoothly.
