Assessing quality and function

Congratulations on finishing your pen! This week is about looking critically at what you have made. A quality pen should write smoothly and feel comfortable to hold. The wood should be flush with the metal parts with no gaps, the finish should be even and durable, and the mechanism should extend and retract the refill reliably. If any part feels loose or rough, identify why and plan how to fix it next time.

Reflection and improvement

Reflection helps you learn from experience. Think back through each stage of the project: preparing the blanks, turning, sanding, finishing and assembling. What steps went well? What challenges did you face, and how did you overcome them? Did you achieve the design you imagined at the start? Consider how you would improve your process or design if you made another pen. Being honest about problems allows you to grow as a maker.

Sustainability and future projects

When choosing materials and finishes for future projects, think about sustainability. Selecting responsibly sourced timber and durable finishes reduces environmental impact.
