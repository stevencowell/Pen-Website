What is a lathe?

A lathe is a machine that spins a piece of wood while you shape it with cutting tools. By holding the tool against the spinning wood you can turn square timber into a round shape for projects like pens, bowls and candlesticks. In this support activity we break down the key points from your first week.

Staying safe

Woodturning can be dangerous if you are not prepared. Always wear personal protective equipment (PPE) such as safety glasses to protect your eyes, a dust mask to avoid breathing dust and hearing protection when the workshop is noisy. Tie back long hair, remove jewellery and avoid loose clothing so nothing can get caught in the spinning work.

Only use the lathe when your teacher says it is safe. Keep the floor clear of tools and scrap wood so no one trips. If you see a spill, clean it up straight away. Never run or play in the workshop. Before you turn on the machine, check that the wood blank is secured between the centres, the tool rest is close to the wood (about 2â€“3Â mm away) and the speed is set correctly for the size of the blank.

Practising on scrap wood

You will start by practising on a scrap piece of timber. This helps you learn how to hold and move the chisels safely. Rest the tool on the tool rest, keep two hands on the handle and move slowly and smoothly. Practise basic cutting, scraping and sanding while the wood spins. When you feel comfortable, you will be ready to turn your actual pen blanks next week.
