Preparing the blanks

Before turning, you need to prepare your pen blanks. Mark a pencil line across the blank, then saw it into two pieces so you can realign the grain later. Drill a hole through each piece using the correct size drill bit. Retract the bit regularly to remove chips so the hole stays clean. Roughen the surface of the brass tubes with sandpaper, apply glue and insert them into the blanks. Allow the glue to dry fully before moving on. Square the ends of the wood so they are flush with the tubes.

Turning on the lathe

Mount the blanks on the mandrel with bushings and tighten the nut gently. Support the end of the mandrel with the tailstock. Turn on the lathe and use a sharp chisel or spindle gouge to round the wood. Work slowly and take light cuts until the blanks are round and close to the bushing diameter. Shape the pen bodies so they are slightly thicker in the middle and flush at the ends. Move through several grits of sandpaper â€“ start with coarse and finish with fine â€“ to make the surface smooth.

Finishing and assembly

Once the blanks are smooth, apply a finish such as wax or friction polish while the lathe spins slowly. Wipe away any excess and let it dry. Lay out the pen parts: nib, clip, mechanism, refill and decorative rings. Press the nib and mechanism into one blank, the clip onto the other blank and finally join the halves together, aligning the grain. Check that the refill extends and retracts properly before presenting your pen.
